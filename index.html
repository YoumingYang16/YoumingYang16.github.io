Project Overview

VisionClimb explores how inclusive interaction design can make indoor climbing more accessible to visually impaired users. Conventional climbing environments depend heavily on visual route cues, spatial recognition, and quick interpretation of wall layouts. Our concept focuses on reducing that reliance through structured guidance, accessible information delivery, and safer real-time support.

Problem Visual route dependency limits independent participation.
Goal Create a climbing experience that feels safer and more understandable.
Method Use accessibility-led thinking, personas, and interaction design direction.

Research Focus

Inclusive Sports Experience Designing for dignity, confidence, and meaningful participation instead of minimal accommodation.
Multimodal Guidance Exploring audio, haptic, and coaching support to communicate route and movement information clearly.
Human-Centered Development Using personas to shape design priorities around real needs, real pain points, and real context.

User Personas

These personas help our team understand the real motivations, frustrations, and context of potential users in an inclusive climbing environment.

Persona 01 · Primary User

Ethan Lin

Age: 21

Background: Ethan is a university student with severe visual impairment. He enjoys physical challenges and wants to participate in indoor climbing, but unfamiliar spaces and visually dependent route systems make the experience intimidating.

Goals:

  • Build confidence in navigating a climbing environment
  • Understand route progression through accessible guidance
  • Participate in sports with more independence and dignity

Pain Points:

  • Cannot rely on visual route markers or wall mapping
  • Feels anxiety in fast-changing or unfamiliar spatial layouts
  • Needs guidance that is timely, clear, and not overwhelming

Technology Habits: Uses screen readers, voice feedback, and accessibility settings daily on his smartphone.

Why He Would Use VisionClimb: It offers structure, clarity, and supportive feedback that can turn climbing into a safer and more empowering experience.

Persona 02 · Support Role

Sophia Chen

Age: 29

Background: Sophia is a climbing coach and accessibility volunteer who supports beginners in adaptive sports settings. She wants reliable tools that improve communication and make coaching for visually impaired users more structured.

Goals:

  • Provide safer and clearer route support during sessions
  • Reduce confusion caused by fast verbal coaching alone
  • Help users feel supported while still developing independence

Pain Points:

  • Real-time direction can be difficult during movement-heavy moments
  • Different users require different levels of support and pacing
  • There are few dedicated tools built for inclusive climbing guidance

Technology Habits: Uses mobile apps, wearables, and digital planning tools for coaching and session management.

Why She Would Use VisionClimb: It improves clarity, supports safer instruction, and helps create a more thoughtful inclusive sports experience.

Design Principles

Our concept direction is guided by a set of design principles that balance accessibility, clarity, and confidence-building interaction.

Accessible by Default

Every touchpoint should reduce cognitive burden and communicate clearly through non-visual channels whenever possible.

Confidence Through Guidance

The system should not only direct movement, but also help users feel secure, supported, and capable throughout the activity.

Human-Centered Inclusion

Design decisions should reflect lived experience, real constraints, and the emotional dimension of participation in adaptive sport.
